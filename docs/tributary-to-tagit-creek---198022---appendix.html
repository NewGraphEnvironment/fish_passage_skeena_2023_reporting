Skeena Watershed Fish Passage Restoration Planning 2023

Tributary to Tagit Creek - 198022 - Appendix

Site Location

PSCIS crossing 198022 and 197949 are located 0.2km and 0.7km upstream of Tagit Creek, respectively, on Tributary to Tagit Creek which flows in a southeast direction. Crossing 198022 is located on 9946-27 Rd and at this location is the responsibility of West Fraser Mills Ltd. Crossing 197949 is located on a Spur of 9946-27 Rd and at this location is the responsibility of the Ministry of Forest. The crossings are located in a very remote area approximately 1h15 drive from Houston, BC.


Background

At both crossing 198022 and 197949, Tributary to Tagit Creek is a second order stream with an upstream watershed area of approximately 4km2. The elevation ranges from a maximum of 1379m to 815m near the crossing (Table 5.1).

In 2021, crossing’s 198022 and 197949 were assessed with a fish passage assessment by Irvine (2021), who observed nice gravels and fish upstream of crossing 197949 and suspected a beaver dam upstream of 198022 due to slow moving water. Upstream of the crossing 198022, cutthroat trout have been documented in the past (Norris [2018] 2024; MoE 2024). In 2023, both sites were prioritized for follow up with a habitat confirmation and fish sampling to investigate conclusions from the 2021 assessment, with results included in this report.

Stream Characteristics at Crossing 198022

During the 2023 survey, PSCIS crossing 198022 was un-embedded, non-backwatered and ranked as a barrier to upstream fish passage according to the provincial protocol (MoE 2011) (Table 5.4). There were signs beaver activity and a large amount of road fill (2m) above the culvert, making for an expensive replacement. The habitat value was rated as medium. Water temperature was 6.5\(^\circ\)C, pH was 6.9 and conductivity was 267uS/cm, which was substantially higher than at crossing 197949 upstream. The crossing was rated as a moderate priority for replacement.

Approximately 175m east on 9946-27 Rd, crossing 2023092701 was also assessed. The crossing was on a small, un-mapped Freshwater Atlas stream that flowed into Tagit Creek. There was wetland habitat upstream and downstream and some blockage at the culvert inlet due to a beaver dam which created a small inlet drop. The culvert was is poor condition with rust and holes but was rated as a low priority for follow up due to its remote location.

Stream Characteristics at Crossing 197949

During the 2023 survey, PSCIS crossing 197949 was un-embedded, non-backwatered and ranked as a barrier to upstream fish passage according to the provincial protocol (MoE 2011) (Table 5.5). The culvert had a 0.5m outlet drop, low flows, and fish were observed in the outlet pool. Similar to the downstream crossing, this crossing had 1.2m of road fill above the culvert, making for an expensive replacement. The habitat value was rated as medium. Water temperature was 7.6\(^\circ\)C, pH was 7.5 and conductivity was 112uS/cm. The crossing was rated as a moderate priority for replacement.

Stream Characteristics Downstream of Crossing 198022

The stream was surveyed downstream from crossing 198022 for 250m , to the confluence with Tagit Creek. The first 100m of stream contained sporadic pools but then dewatered. The stream flowed through a steep confined valley to Tagit Creek and at the confluence there was a large log jam (Figure 5.2). Total cover amount was rated as moderate with large woody debris dominant. Cover was also present as small woody debris and overhanging vegetation.The dominant substrate was cobbles with gravels sub-dominant.The average channel width was 1.8m, the average wetted width was 0.2m, and the average gradient was 3%. The habitat was rated as medium value for salmonid rearing and spawning.


Stream Characteristics Upstream of Crossing 198022 and Downstream of Crossing 197949

The stream was surveyed from the upper crossing 197949 downstream to the lower crossing 198022, approximately 500m (Figure 5.4). At the top end of the site there were occasional shallow pools due to steeper gradients. Surveyors observed several fish in the outlet pool of the upper crossing. Approximately 75m downstream of crossing 197949, the stream dewatered but contained abundant gravels. The remainder of the stream down to crossing 198022 was a wetland type area, dominated by willow and sedge, with the main channel being difficult to find. The average channel width was 2.5m, the average wetted width was 0.3m, and the average gradient was 3.8%.Total cover amount was rated as moderate with overhanging vegetation dominant. Cover was also present as small woody debris and undercut banks.The dominant substrate was gravels with fines sub-dominant. The habitat was rated as medium for salmonid rearing and spawning.


Stream Characteristics Upstream of Crossing 197949

The stream was surveyed upstream from crossing 197949 for 550m . The channel was small with low flows and occasional dewatered patches (Figure 5.5). There were very few deep pools but fish were observed as far as 500m upstream of the crossing. The dominant substrate was cobbles with gravels sub-dominant.Total cover amount was rated as abundant with overhanging vegetation dominant. Cover was also present as small woody debris and large woody debris.The average channel width was 2.3m, the average wetted width was 1m, and the average gradient was 6.8%. The habitat was rated as medium for salmonid rearing and spawning.


Fish Sampling

In 2023, electrofishing was conducted above and below crossing 198022 with the upstream site located just below crossing 197949. The results are summarised in Tables 5.7 - 5.8 and Figure 5.1. A total of 17 cutthroat were captured upstream, and 1 dolly varden was captured downstream (Figures 5.6 - 5.7). All fish captured with a fork length greater than 60mm were tagged with Passive Integrated Transponders (PIT tags) with data stored here.


Structure Remediation and Cost Estimate

Should restoration/maintenance activities proceed, it is recommended that both crossings be each replaced with a bridge (15m span). The preliminary cost estimate for each bridge is $450,000, with the relatively high work costs related to the large amount of road fill present at the sites and the subsequent significant excavation required to install the large replacement structure.


Conclusion

PSCIS crossings 198022 and 197949 are located in a remote area of the Morice watershed and both have a large amount of road fill above the culvert, making for expensive replacements. Nevertheless, electrofishing in 2023 confirmed the stream provides valuable habitat to cutthroat trout, suggesting the stream may be important for spawning and rearing salmonids. The habitat value was rated as medium for both crossings, with moderate priority for replacement.
